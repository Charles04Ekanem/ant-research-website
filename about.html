About

ANT R&D (Artificially (e)Ngineering Technologies) is a privately-led research and innovation lab based in Akwa Ibom, Nigeria. We are committed to developing original, real-world technologies rooted in Nigerian contexts—particularly through artificial intelligence, embedded systems, and smart signal solutions.

We build from the ground up, often working with noisy, low-resource or underrepresented datasets that reflect everyday Nigerian realities. At ANT R&D, innovation is local, practical and purpose-driven.

Vision and Mission

Vision: To be a beacon of grassroots innovation in Nigeria—designing technologies that speak to our world, our people, and our problems.

Mission:

  • To create practical, ethically collected datasets and tools that reflect Nigerian realities
  • To design AI models, smart embedded systems and signal solutions tailored to local use-cases
  • To foster open collaboration, knowledge sharing and ethical research in underserved communities

Founder's Note – Charles Ekanem

Welcome to ANT R&D.
This lab was born out of necessity. Too often, the global AI and tech ecosystem builds for environments that look nothing like ours. As a Black Nigerian, I see firsthand how our voices, data and contexts are underrepresented in the global world of technology.

ANT R&D is my personal response; a commitment to research, build and publish tools and models that start from our realities. We don’t just localize global tools; we invent locally.

— Charles Ekanem

Location

Uyo, Akwa Ibom State, Nigeria

Operations fully remote-capable; collaboration welcome across Nigeria and beyond.

Focus Areas

  • Artificial Intelligence (AI): Real-world, small-scale, noise-tolerant models
  • Embedded Systems: Low-cost smart solutions for Nigerian environments
  • Signal Processing: Algorithms for audio, image and biometric data in real-world noisy settings
  • Data Engineering: Ethical, localized dataset creation with open licensing
  • Research & Publications: Deepfake detection, Agritech, Nigerian-centered datasets

Philosophy

  • Ethical Approach: Every dataset we collect and create requires informed consent and a clear license.
  • Inclusion: We aim to include underrepresented voices; dialect-limited, education-limited.
  • Dataset Realism: Noise is a feature. Our datasets include real-world imperfections.
  • Local-First Innovation: We design from the local outward.
